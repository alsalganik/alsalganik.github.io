Accreting Neutron Stars

Observational constraints on how strongly magnetized neutron stars capture matter and radiate in X-rays.

The site brings together papers and observing programs that focus on accretion onto neutron stars with strong magnetic fields. The recurring questions are how accretion columns form, how the flow changes with luminosity, and how the resulting geometry shapes the measured timing and spectral signals.

Focus on this site

  • Accretion regimes in magnetized compact objects
  • Links between luminosity changes and source state changes
  • Comparative work across persistent and transient systems
