X-ray Pulsars

Pulse-period evolution, accretion states, and high-energy emission from strongly magnetized neutron stars.

X-ray pulsars are central to this research profile. The work on this site studies how strongly magnetized neutron stars accrete, switch states, and produce structured high-energy emission that can be traced through timing, spectroscopy, and polarization.

Focus on this site

  • Timing transitions and quasi-periodic variability
  • Broadband observational constraints on accretion geometry
  • Long-period and transient pulsars observed with modern X-ray missions
